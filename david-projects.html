Simple Bible - A Lightweight Bible App

The first Android App I finished, SimpleBible is now in closed testing on Google Play! The purpose of this app was to deliver a Bible application that is lightweight, add-free, free to use, and fully offline without any quirks (like collecting your email). Despite the variety of Bible apps available on the Play Store, very few can check all 4 of those boxes. I wanted something simple and small, and figured there are other folks who are down for an app like that too (turns out there are at least a few!). The app currently supports only the King James Version of the Bible. I do intend on bringing other versions of the Bible that are in the public domain to it eventually.

While this app served as a springboard for diving into Android development, I still have a few more plans for it before making a version 1.0 publically available. In addition, my goal is to seek after books in the public domain and get them formatted to work with the reader I built for this app.

Turing Machine Simulator

Every since I took Automata Theory, I was fascinated with turing machines. I liked them so much so, that I built a simple to use turing machine simulator. This turing machine simulator has its own language and it shows the cells and head as it moves around.

This turing machine isn't a regular turing machine, but rather a variant, but it's quite close to a pure turing machine.

If you can write a program to compute something, a turing machine can compute it too. If you CAN'T right a program for something, neither can a turing machine.
