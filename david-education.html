Teaching Fellowship

Since the fall of 2022, I've been a teaching fellow for CMSC201 (Introduction to Computer Science, taught using Python). As a teaching fellow, I have several responsibilities. First of all, I'm responsibible for hosting a lab session each week. In these lab sessions, I go over the material designated for that week and give the students an exercise and assess their ability and help them as needed. The next major responsibility is hosting office hours. For several hours a week I'm in the designated room helping students with homeworks, projects, labs and general questions on the material. The third major responsibility is grading assignments. This is done through automated scripts, manual testing, and code reviewing. In addition, I am also occasionally responsible for exam proctoring, and responding to student inquiries via email. Being a teacing fellow has given me valuable skills in making hard concepts easy, communicating, and organizing. There is a great deal of discretion given to teaching fellows, and thus a responsibility to be fair and do one's best at it. Being a teaching fellow also enabled me to attain the Undergraduate CIRTL associate certification, a culmination of 2 semesters of learning about teaching, learning, and research methods, and ultimately applying them to a small semester long research project based in my and my groupmates lab sections.
